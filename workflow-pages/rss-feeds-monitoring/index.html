The RSS feed monitoring playbook

What is an RSS feed monitoring workflow?

An RSS feed is a live list of new articles a website publishes. A monitoring workflow is the automated system that watches those feeds around the clock and reads every new one the second it goes live.

Companies signal that they are about to buy long before they reach your site, in the news, in press releases, in industry coverage. Each of those moments is a buying signal, and by the time someone on your team stumbles across one, a competitor has usually already called.

This workflow turns that public noise into pipeline. It catches a signal within hours, pulls out the details, finds the right people, and hands a rep a qualified lead while the need is still fresh.

You stop emailing cold strangers and start reaching the right company at the exact moment it is looking. And because it runs inside your own tools, the pipeline stays yours to keep.

What you can monitor

Every buying signal hiding in public news

You choose the signals that predict a need for what you sell. These are the public moments a monitoring workflow catches, and what each one tells you.

Use cases

Funding rounds

A fresh raise means budget that has to be deployed, fast. New tools, new hires, and new vendors follow within weeks. Catch the announcement and you reach them while the spending plan is still being written.

Leadership changes

A new VP or C-level hire arrives with a mandate to change things. They audit the existing stack, question old vendor relationships, and bring their own preferences. The first 90 days is when those decisions get made.

Hiring surges

A wave of open roles is a team scaling faster than its tools can keep up. Whatever that department needs to support the headcount, they are buying it now. The job posts tell you exactly which function is under pressure.

Office moves and expansions

A new location or a signed lease sets off a chain of operational decisions: facilities, services, security, connectivity. Each one is a budget line opening up, and most vendors never hear about it in time.

Product launches

A launch is a company betting on growth, and growth needs support. New markets, new channels, and new infrastructure all get bought to back it. The press release is your cue to arrive with the thing they suddenly need.

Mergers and acquisitions

Two companies becoming one means duplicate tools, clashing systems, and a hard deadline to consolidate. Whoever helps them standardize wins a big, urgent project. The deal announcement is the starting gun.

Whatever you sell, some public moment means a company is about to need it. You define which signals count.

Questions we get asked

What is an RSS feed monitoring workflow?

It is an automated system that watches the news, press-release, and industry feeds you choose, reads every new article the moment it publishes, and turns the relevant ones into qualified leads in your CRM. It runs around the clock, so your team catches buying signals within hours instead of stumbling on them weeks later.

What tools does it use?

A typical build uses n8n to watch the feeds, Clay and its Claygent AI to read and enrich each signal, a contact tool like LeadsFactory.io to find decision-makers, and your CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive) as the destination. Outreach can run through Slack, Instantly, and HeyReach. The exact set depends on your stack.

Can I build this myself?

Yes. Everything here runs on tools you can buy. The hard part is wiring them together, writing the scoring and keyword rules, and maintaining it as feeds and APIs change. Most teams would rather have it built and tuned for them, then own the result.

Does it work with my CRM?

Yes. It writes leads into HubSpot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive, with deduplication so monitoring at scale never clutters your database. If you run a different CRM, it can usually connect too.

How accurate is it, and what about false positives?

Every signal is read in full and cross-checked against the wider web before it moves forward, which filters out most false positives. The keyword rules and scoring are tuned to your ICP during setup, and they keep improving as real reply data comes in.

How long does it take to set up?

About a month from kickoff to live. Roughly a week to define your ICP and signals and stand up the infrastructure, two weeks to build and tune the engine, and a final stretch to wire activation and launch.

Do I own the system?

Yes. It is built inside your own accounts and tools, so the pipeline it creates stays yours. There is no platform to rent and nothing to lose if you ever part ways with us.
