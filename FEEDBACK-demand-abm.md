# Delivery-team feedback · Demand gen/ABM page
Source: "website visual fedback" Google Doc, Demand gen/ABM tab (read 2026-07-03).
Applies to: the Demand gen / ABM service page (not yet built in this sandbox).
Mirror of the Revenue/GTM philosophy — hub-not-funnel, dimming = credibility,
tools recede to faint labels, engine at the hub, three strategic decisions at one altitude.

## The three banners — demand arc: generate → concentrate → convert

### Visual 1 — Generate (left) → WHERE DEMAND COMES FROM
- About demand creation AND capture: where demand comes from for this buyer and how
  we catch it the moment it appears. Currently MISSING from the page — closest material
  is buried in the "Inbound Lead Capture" capability panel; lift it up here.
- NOT a left-to-right funnel (reads as "a pipe things pass through"). Build as a HUB with
  lines radiating out to demand sources around it, us in the middle. Center = "we
  orchestrate the whole demand system." (Similar visual to Sales Engine banner 1.)
- Two kinds of spokes, showing both is the point:
  - CREATE demand: paid, content, SEO, events
  - CATCH demand: direct traffic, inbound, intent signals
  Same hub, two kinds of spokes = "generate and capture" in one shape.
- HERO = what travels inward and resolves at the hub: anonymous signals becoming a named
  account. Visitor off paid line + form-fill off inbound line + intent spike off signals
  line → all arrive at center → resolve into "Morrow Labs · 84 employees · logistics SaaS
  · scored 94." Motion is inward-and-resolving, not outward-and-broadcasting.
- Trap to avoid: spokes are demand SOURCES, not tool logos (don't repeat the logo-cloud).
  Tools = faint side-labels at most. Keep live feel (green pill, signal ticking in).

### Visual 2 — Concentrate (middle) — THE HEAVIEST BANNER → WHICH ACCOUNTS GET MORE
- This is ABM, the star of the page — most visual weight of the three. Strategic decision:
  which accounts deserve more than everyone else, and how firepower gets coordinated.
- This is the current "ABM operating model" banner — rescue it from the logo-cloud trap
  (currently ICP node ringed by tool logos + tier list = shows toolset, not what ABM does).
- HERO = COORDINATION, not a tier breakdown. One target account in the center getting a
  coordinated motion firing in concert — LinkedIn + email + display ad + AE outreach — all
  aimed at that one account, same story across every channel. Set against it: "everyone
  else" getting broad/generic treatment, visibly DIMMED. The dimming is the credibility
  move (same as channel-strategy on Sales Engine) — a broad-campaign shop can't do it.
- Show only accounts/channels we'd actually run — believable real enterprise example, not
  a five-channel orchestra we don't operate.
- Tier breakdown (Tier 1: 24, Tier 2: 86, Tier 3: 237) can stay as small supporting
  structure down the side — NOT the hero.
- Live audience-syncing lands HERE, as one channel in the coordinated play, not its own banner.

### Visual 3 — Convert (right) → HOW IT BECOMES PIPELINE
- Demand becoming pipeline: how generated + concentrated demand turns into real revenue.
- Replaces the weak "audiences syncing" banner; absorbs the current funnel-workflow
  material (Awareness → MQL → SQL → Won, deal value) — always closer to "becomes pipeline."
- HERO = the source/signal stamp riding through to the pipeline number: "€514k pipeline ·
  tied to source, signal, sequence" — deliberately echoes capability 09 on Sales Engine so
  the pages rhyme.
- Two soft sideways nods (flow, not redirect): "feeds your engine's pipeline," "rolls up
  in your RevOps backbone." Closes the page back into the rest of the company.
- Same card chrome, green pill, live feel. Avoid vanity-metric read (not impressions/clicks
  — pipeline tied back to where it came from).

Arc summary: catch the demand → concentrate it on the accounts that matter → turn it into
pipeline. ABM heaviest in the middle. Audience-syncing was pulled from a pillar slot (it's
execution, one channel inside ABM, not a strategic decision) → now lives inside Concentrate.
Makes us read as a growth partner running one demand system, not an ad-ops shop syncing lists.

## Demand Gen / ABM — nine capabilities
Same rules as Sales Engine: technical titles (signal expertise) + plain-language one-liner +
visual carries the meaning. Tools recede to faint labels. Green pill + live counters. ABM (06)
visibly heaviest. Fix the two panels that currently DUPLICATE the Sales Engine page (capture,
scoring) so a prospect never sees the same screen twice. Order does work: strategy defines
where to look → engine runs plays → generate/concentrate/convert. Nothing captures blind.

- **01 — Demand Strategy & Channel Mapping.** "We figure out where your buyers actually hang
  out and what they do right before they're ready to buy — so you're not guessing where to
  show up." Visual: a MAP not a list. Buyer in middle; channels ranked by where this audience
  shows demand — LinkedIn + events lit as primary, poor-fit dimmed "low signal here." Intent
  signals (pricing-page visit, competitor comparison, hiring) called out as triggers. Hero =
  the decision. Absorbs old ICP panel data (Series B/C, 4,212 TAM, 312 in-market) as a
  foundation strip. Same energy as Sales Engine TAM funnel.
- **02 — AI-Powered Demand Workflows & Plays — THE ANCHOR, biggest panel.** "A set of
  automations that spot interest the moment it happens and act on it instantly — the same
  engine that runs your outbound, now working to bring demand in." Visual: several plays
  running AT ONCE (not one linear flow) — intent-triggered play, competitor-signal play,
  event-follow-up play, nurture play — each with its own status ticking. "Many plays, one
  engine" — visually separates it from the Sales Engine engine panel while staying clearly
  the same engine. Most-alive panel on the page.
- **03 — Audience Building & Paid Media Strategy.** "We build ad audiences from your own
  customer data, not random targeting — so your ad spend reaches the right companies, and you
  see the pipeline it creates, not just clicks." Visual: lead with the AUDIENCE as the asset —
  "Tier-1 ABM list · lookalikes from closed-won · CRM-suppression" — then channel spend bars
  (Google/LinkedIn/Meta) underneath, ending on "€128k spend → €312k influenced pipeline · 2.4×
  ROAS." Hero = influenced pipeline, not spend.
- **04 — Social Listening & Competitive Tracking.** "We watch for the moment a target company
  complains about a problem you solve or shops a competitor — and catch them while they're
  looking." Visual: keep current (it's good) — captured quotes ("Workato vs Tray," "need
  better lead routing," "hiring 3 SDRs") tagged by signal strength, ending on "signal captured
  → auto-brief." Give it slightly more room; underweighted for how distinctive it is.
- **05 — Inbound Capture & Dynamic Landing Pages.** "When someone visits your site, we work
  out which company they're from and show them a page tailored to them — turning anonymous
  traffic into named accounts you can act on." Visual: two beats. The resolve — anonymous
  visitor becomes "Morrow Labs · 84 emp · logistics SaaS" — but ending on the account ENTERING
  PIPELINE, NOT "routed to Sara in 38s" (that routing line is the exact duplicate of the Sales
  Engine page; killing it stops the two pages showing the same screen). Then the adaptive
  landing page ("personalized for Logistics · smart form · stitched to account record"). The
  stitch-to-record is the connective detail.
- **06 — Account-Based Marketing & ABM Programs — THE STAR, heaviest card.** "For the handful
  of accounts that really matter, we run a coordinated push across email, LinkedIn, ads and
  your reps — all at once, all saying the same thing — instead of treating them like everyone
  else." Visual: keep current (best on the page) — LinkedIn ad + personalized email + dynamic
  landing page + SDR task, "4 channels in lockstep," "rep activated when intent threshold
  hits." Make it visibly the heaviest of the nine.
- **07 — Nurture & Lifecycle Journeys.** "We keep warm leads warm — following up based on what
  they actually do, not a fixed schedule — so nobody who was interested slips through the
  cracks." Visual (new): a journey that BRANCHES on behaviour, not a linear drip. "Opened +
  visited pricing → advance," "went quiet 14 days → re-engage," "hit intent threshold → hand
  to sales." Branching = intelligent, not a scheduled chain.
- **08 — Inbound-Led Outbound Sequences.** "The second someone shows real interest, we reach
  out with a personal message while it's still hot — instead of waiting days and losing the
  moment." Visual: keep current but lead on the TRIGGER — "website visit → intent threshold met
  →" as opening beat, then the sequence. Drop the "domain healthy" line (Sales Engine
  deliverability plumbing). End on handoff: "your engine picks it up the moment intent hits."
- **09 — Pipeline & Attribution Reporting.** "You can see exactly which campaigns brought in
  real pipeline and revenue — not vanity numbers like impressions and clicks." Visual:
  pipeline-by-source bars (mirrors Sales Engine 09 so pages rhyme), each stamped with source
  and signal, ending on total influenced pipeline. Handoff chip: "we stamp every campaign ·
  full attribution rolls up on your RevOps backbone."

## Flywheel (bottom) — deliberately the SAME flywheel as Sales Engine
- Same shape, hub, and loop-back as the Sales Engine flywheel — that sameness IS the "one
  engine, three services" story told visually. Build as one shared component with labels
  swapped per page and the continuity does the work for free.
- Engine at the hub (same engine, now showing "plays running · €312k pipeline" instead of
  "23 booked"). Generate → Concentrate → Convert cycling around it. Six steps nested
  two-per-phase. Amber LOOP-BACK carrying "what converts sharpens next round" back through
  the engine to Generate.
- Only the WORDS differ from Sales Engine; shape/hub/loop-back identical.
- Same caveats: direction sketch not final asset — needs counter ticking, phases lighting on
  scroll, one faint source per step, running pill; only reads as "compounds" when it visibly
  turns (a static flywheel dies). "Three loud, six quiet" discipline — six steps stay tiny
  text inside phases; the moment they grow into their own nodes it's back to a six-node mess.
- OPEN DECISION (author flagged, needs Ibrahim's call): the loop-back fuel. Sales Engine used
  "booked meetings." Here the author used "what converts" (pipeline) as the fuel that sharpens
  the next Generate. Alternative: loop-back carries INTENT SIGNALS instead of converted
  pipeline (signals feed back faster/more often). Pipeline = stronger business story (loops on
  the outcome that matters); signals = more technically-alive (loops constantly). Author's lean
  = pipeline, to match Sales Engine logic and keep them symmetric — but flagged for Ibrahim.
