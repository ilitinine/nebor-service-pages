01 Week 1–2

Audit

Full data, schema, and workflow audit. We catalogue the duplicates, drift, and dead ends before touching anything.

What we'll need

02 Week 3–6

Re-engineer

Object model, lifecycle, hygiene, enrichment, and routing rebuilt around how your team actually sells.

You'll see
  • Cleaned + deduped data with a reviewable change log
  • New lifecycle stages, scoring, and routing rules going live
  • Continuous enrichment hydrating accounts on every write
What we'll need
  • Admin rights on CRM + permission to wire integrations

03 Week 6–9

Embed

You'll see

What we'll need
  • 2 hours from each RevOps operator for live training
  • One person designated as the long-term system owner
  • Final sign-off on data definitions + reporting

FAQ

Things teams ask before they start.

Will you replace our CRM? +
No. We work with what you have · HubSpot, Salesforce, or otherwise · and optimize. We're not here to rip and replace; we're here to make your existing stack do what it should.
How long does a full cleanup take? +
Initial hygiene typically lands in 2–4 weeks. The harder work is wiring the systems so it stays clean · that's an ongoing build measured in weeks, not quarters.
Do we need a data warehouse? +
Not always. Many teams can run RevOps entirely inside the CRM. When you outgrow that, we layer in Snowflake or BigQuery and wire reverse-ETL back into the CRM · but only when it earns its complexity.
Who owns the system once you're done? +
You do. Always. We document, train, and hand over. The infrastructure compounds because your team operates it · not because we keep the lights on.
How do you handle data that's already in the CRM? +
We audit first, then dedupe, normalize, and enrich systematically. Nothing is deleted without a reviewable backup, and every cleanup pass is logged so you can see exactly what changed and roll back if needed.
What's the difference between RevOps and SalesOps? +
SalesOps focuses on the sales motion · quotas, pipeline, comp. RevOps spans sales + marketing + customer-success and owns the data layer underneath. We build for RevOps because that's where compounding actually lives.
Can you connect the CRM to our product/usage data? +
Yes. We pipe product events through Segment or directly via API into the CRM, model them in dbt or BigQuery, and surface usage signals on the right account record so reps see who's hot.
How do you measure success for a RevOps engagement? +
Three things: data completeness (target 95%+ post-cleanup), workflow uptime (no manual handoffs), and rep adoption (CRM is the system reps actually open). We instrument and report on all three weekly.
What if our team is non-technical? +
Most aren't. Our handoff includes screen-recorded runbooks, a one-pager per workflow, and a 60-minute live training. The system is operated through the CRM UI itself · your team doesn't need to read code.
